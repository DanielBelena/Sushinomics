The Beckham Law versus standard income tax

How much tax you pay on your gross salary in Catalonia depending on which regime you fall under, from €30,000 to €100,000 of annual gross pay. When the six years of the special expat regime run out, you switch to the ordinary progressive scale — this is the difference.

Marginal rate vs effective rate. Tax tables usually quote the marginal rate — the percentage charged on your very last euro of income (44% in Catalonia once you pass €60,000). But you don't pay that on your whole salary. Each slice of income is taxed at its own bracket rate, and the first slices are cheap. The effective rate is the total tax divided by the full salary, which is always lower. That effective rate is what you can fairly compare against Beckham's flat 24%.

Adding social security

Income tax isn't the whole story. Both regimes also pay employee social security — and it's the same amount, since it's the same job under the same Spanish contract. But there's a twist: those contributions are tax-deductible under the standard regime and not under Beckham. That one difference reshapes the comparison at the lower end.

Total take from your pay: tax + social security

For the exact figures with social security, switch on “Include social security” in the table above.

The break-even point moves up. Without social security, Beckham is cheaper at every salary in this range. Add it, and the standard regime's deduction makes it the better deal up to roughly €40,000 — at €30,000 Beckham actually costs about €565 more. Above that, Beckham pulls ahead again and the gap keeps widening, reaching about €10,400 at €100,000. The advantage keeps growing because social security is capped at €58,914: beyond that it's a flat ≈€3,820 for everyone, while Beckham's flat 24% keeps beating the rising marginal rates.

How it's calculated

  • Standard income tax: the 2025 official scales are applied to the gross salary, summing the state scale (art. 63.1 of the Income Tax Act) and the Catalan regional scale from Decree-Law 5/2025 (art. 611-1). In part one no deductions are applied; in part two, social security is deducted from the tax base.
  • Beckham Law: a flat 24% on the first €600,000 (47% on the excess, which never applies in this range), applied to the gross salary with no deductions.
  • Social security (part two): the 2025 employee rate of 6.48% on the contribution base, capped at the 2025 maximum of €58,914/year. Assumed identical under both regimes (same Spanish general-regime contract). The small solidarity surcharge on pay above the cap is left out as immaterial to the comparison.
  • Part one leaves out social security, the personal and family allowance, and the earned-income reduction, so the figures match a simple online calculator. Those reductions would lower the real standard-regime tax somewhat, especially at lower salaries.
Indicative calculation, not tax advice. Scales in force for the 2025 tax year (filed in 2026), for a taxpayer resident in Catalonia.
