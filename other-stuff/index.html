The Beckham Law versus standard income tax

How much tax you pay on your gross salary in Catalonia depending on which regime you fall under, from €30,000 to €100,000 of annual gross pay. When the six years of the special expat regime run out, you switch to the ordinary progressive scale — this is the difference.

Marginal rate vs effective rate. Tax tables usually quote the marginal rate — the percentage charged on your very last euro of income (44% in Catalonia once you pass €60,000). But you don't pay that on your whole salary. Each slice of income is taxed at its own bracket rate, and the first slices are cheap. The effective rate is the total tax divided by the full salary, which is always lower. That effective rate is what you can fairly compare against Beckham's flat 24%.

How it's calculated

  • Standard income tax: the 2025 official scales are applied directly to the gross salary, summing the state scale (art. 63.1 of the Income Tax Act) and the Catalan regional scale from Decree-Law 5/2025 (art. 611-1).
  • Beckham Law: a flat 24% on the first €600,000 (47% on the excess, which never applies in this range).
  • Not included: social security contributions, the personal and family allowance, or the earned-income reduction. The goal is a simple figure you can check against online calculators. Applying those reductions would lower the real income tax somewhat, especially at lower salaries.
